# Not The Rug — Copy Audit, Gap Analysis & Strategy Priority Document

**Purpose**: This document serves three functions:
1. **Section A — Copy Mapping**: Maps every content area of the existing site to its equivalent in the new site
2. **Section B — Gap Analysis**: Flags copy, facts, and details present on the existing site that are missing or inaccurate in the new site
3. **Section C — Strategy Priority List**: What is working and not working on the old site, to inform how we write and structure copy in the new site

> **Editorial rule**: The new site strategy takes priority. Old site copy should be *considered*, not copied. Where the old copy is stronger, factually grounded, or irreplaceable (e.g. real pricing, staff bios, owner story), it should be adapted into the new site's voice.

# SECTION C — STRATEGY PRIORITY LIST
*What is working and not working on the old website, to influence copy and strategy on the new site*

---

## ✅ WHAT IS WORKING ON THE OLD SITE
*Retain, adapt, or amplify in the new site*

---

### C1. The Founder Story Is the Most Powerful Asset — and It's Underused
**What works**: Luis's background — SiriusXM, Red Bull, left broadcasting to walk dogs — is a remarkable story that instantly elevates Not The Rug above every gig-worker competitor. It explains *why* someone would do this with this level of care.
**Problem on old site**: The story exists on About Us and Our Staff, but it's buried in long prose and doesn't appear on the homepage.
**New site strategy**: Surface the founder story earlier and more visually. The Origin Story section on the About page is great — but a shortened version should appear on the homepage and in the footer or trust bar.

---

### C2. The Walk Philosophy Copy Is Genuinely Differentiated
**What works**: The second homepage paragraph on the old site — about structured walks, reading dog signals, the transition from walk to rest — is the best writing on the entire site. It speaks directly to what anxious dog owners worry about.
**Problem on old site**: It's buried as a second paragraph below generic mission copy, with no visual hierarchy.
**New site strategy**: Extract this language and elevate it. Use it in the How It Works page, in the About values section, or as a pull quote. Don't let it disappear.

---

### C3. Specificity in Safety Copy Builds Trust
**What works**: The FAQ answer about safety equipment is unusually specific — Geartac belts, weekly gear checks, no-phone policy, 4-week new-hire training. Clients reading this feel something real is happening.
**Problem on old site**: It's on the FAQ page, which most visitors never read.
**New site strategy**: Pull the specifics into the Safety & Trust page. The new site's trust cards are currently too vague ("we use safety equipment"). Replace generic claims with the actual equipment names and protocols.

---

### C4. "No Pack-Walking" Is a High-Converting Differentiator
**What works**: The three-dog maximum is mentioned everywhere on the old site — and every real review references trust as the reason for choosing Not The Rug. The no-pack-walk policy is the most direct expression of that trust.
**Problem on old site**: It's stated but not explained in terms of *what it means for the dog*.
**New site strategy**: The new site's "Small Groups, Real Attention" value copy does this well — keep it and sharpen it. Add to service card descriptions where relevant.

---

### C5. The Real Reviews Are Better Than the Placeholder Reviews
**What works**: Jayne A.'s review is exceptional — two-year relationship, surgery rehab assist, text updates and pics, willing to carry the dog outside. This is a word-of-mouth gold narrative.
**Problem on old site**: Only 3 reviews shown on homepage, 4 on testimonials page. Not enough social proof volume for a 15-year business.
**New site strategy**: Replace all placeholder reviews with real ones. The new Reviews page's masonry layout can hold 9–12 real reviews. Pull from Yelp and Google. Make Jayne A.'s review a featured/hero review.

---

### C6. Transparent Pricing Drives Conversions
**What works**: The old site lists every price clearly and directly — no "contact us for pricing" anywhere. This is the single biggest conversion driver for service businesses.
**Problem on old site**: Prices are in a long scrolling list without visual hierarchy or packaging framing.
**New site strategy**: The new site's pricing grid is a major improvement. Keep the price transparency, add the monthly package framing, and make sure tax disclosure is correct.

---

### C7. "Approved Medication" and Medical Care Is a Client Need Signal
**What works**: Almost every service listing on the old site mentions handling "feeding and approved medication." This signal catches clients with dogs who have health needs — senior dogs, post-surgery dogs — and tells them they're covered.
**Problem on old site**: Not visually highlighted — it's buried in service descriptions.
**New site strategy**: Restore "approved medication" to all relevant service descriptions. Consider adding it to the Safety & Trust page as a formal capability statement.

---

### C8. The Team Depth Is a Major Trust Signal Being Wasted
**What works**: The old Our Staff page has 9 detailed, human bios with real personality. Joseph's dog is named Ginger. Ivan has a parrot. Lincoln grew up with emus. These details make the team feel real.
**Problem on old site**: Staff page is one of the least-linked, least-visible pages. No staff photos on the homepage. Team depth is invisible to most visitors.
**New site strategy**: The new About page featuring walkers with photos and bios is the right move. Expand from 4 to all 9. Use actual names and real details (Ginger, Miso, Casper the parrot). Real personality bios convert — they give clients something to connect with before the meet & greet.

---

## ❌ WHAT IS NOT WORKING ON THE OLD SITE
*Do not repeat in the new site*

---

### C9. "E-mail Us" as the Primary CTA Is a Booking Killer
**Problem**: Every single service CTA on the old site says "E-mail Us." There is no online booking, no form pre-populated with service context, no scheduling widget. Clients who want to book have to initiate an unstructured email conversation.
**Impact**: High drop-off. Anyone who found Not The Rug via search, compared it to Rover (instant booking), and chose to email Luis is self-selecting for patience and commitment. This is a significant conversion loss.
**New site strategy**: The phased booking approach (Phase 1: contact form → Phase 2: Time To Pet integration) directly addresses this. The Meet & Greet form with service pre-selection is the right first step.

---

### C10. No Visual Hierarchy on Services — Everything Looks Equal
**Problem**: The old services page is a flat list of service descriptions with prices, separated only by headings. There's no visual indication of what's popular, what's best value, or what to choose first.
**Impact**: Decision fatigue. Clients who are unsure of what they need face a wall of options with no guidance.
**New site strategy**: The new pricing grid with "Most Requested" badge, featured card scaling, and monthly packages is the right approach. The hierarchy guides decision-making without removing options.

---

### C11. Boarding Description Has Wrong Copy (Paste Error)
**Problem**: The Boarding or Dog Sitting section on the old site describes puppy socialization and housebreaking — copy that was clearly pasted from the Puppy Visits section. This is a factual error that undermines credibility.
**New site strategy**: New boarding copy ("In-home overnight care so your dog sleeps in familiar surroundings") is already better. Do not port the old boarding copy.

---

### C12. FAQ Format Reads as Internal Policy, Not Client-Friendly Communication
**Problem**: The old FAQ questions are phrased from the company's point of view — "Why we walk three dogs max?" instead of "Why do you walk only three dogs at a time?" This creates distance between the reader and the business.
**Impact**: Questions that should build trust read as corporate policy statements.
**New site strategy**: The new Safety & Trust page's card format ("Max 3 Dogs Per Walk") and FAQ accordion ("Will my dog always have the same walker?") are properly client-oriented. Keep the phrasing second-person and client-centric.

---

### C13. Williamsburg-Only Positioning Limits Growth
**Problem**: The old FAQ explicitly states "Why we are located only in Williamsburg?" as a feature — and the answer emphasizes staying small and community-rooted. While authentic, this actively discourages inquiry from neighboring areas.
**Impact**: Greenpoint, Bushwick, Bed-Stuy, and Park Slope clients who would otherwise book are told they're out of scope.
**New site strategy**: The neighborhood expansion to 6 areas is the right call. The neighborhood-specific landing pages both serve existing demand and capture local SEO. The FAQ answer should be retired or rewritten to reflect the expanded service area.

---

### C14. Gallery Page Has Zero Copy — Missed SEO and Conversion Opportunity
**Problem**: The Gallery page is image-only with no text, no context, no captions, no CTAs. It is essentially invisible to search engines and does nothing for conversion.
**Impact**: Dead page from an SEO and conversion perspective. Yelp has 162 photos; Instagram has 1,321 posts — the photos exist but aren't being used strategically.
**New site strategy**: The new site removes a dedicated gallery page and distributes photography into neighborhood pages and the homepage hero. This is the correct call. Add photo captions to neighborhood pages for SEO value.

---

### C15. Contact Hours Are Too Narrow (Mon–Fri 10am–6pm Only)
**Problem**: Dog walking is a 7-day-a-week business. Clients looking for weekend coverage, or working professionals trying to book outside business hours, see no weekend availability.
**Impact**: Potential clients assume the service doesn't operate on weekends. The existing site doesn't mention weekend availability anywhere.
**New site strategy**: Verify and post accurate, expanded hours. Even if booking hours are weekday-only, the new contact page's expanded hours (Sat–Sun 9–5) should be confirmed and published.

---

### C16. No Neighborhood SEO Infrastructure
**Problem**: The current site is one domain with no neighborhood-targeted pages, content, or local keywords beyond "Williamsburg." Despite serving clients in Greenpoint and Bushwick, there is zero search presence for "dog walker Greenpoint" or "dog walking Bushwick Brooklyn."
**Impact**: Invisible to ~60% of potential clients who are searching by neighborhood
