# Not The Rug Website Feature Requirements

Source: [research.md](/Users/bballi/Documents/Repos/NotTheRug/docs/research.md)

## Goal

Build a modern marketing and booking website for Not The Rug that:

- increases qualified leads and booked walks
- makes trust and safety obvious
- supports recurring dog-walking revenue
- improves local visibility in Williamsburg and nearby neighborhoods
- gives the brand a more memorable, human voice

## Product Priorities

The research points to four core business problems:

1. Booking is too manual and creates friction.
2. The current site does not fully communicate trust, safety, and professionalism.
3. Local SEO is underdeveloped for Williamsburg and surrounding neighborhoods.
4. The brand story and team personality are not being used to differentiate from Rover, Wag, and other competitors.

The phased plan below turns those findings into an implementation order.

## Phase 1: Launch-Critical Marketing Site

Objective: ship a polished, mobile-friendly site that explains the service clearly and drives consultation and booking intent.

### Required pages

- Homepage
- Services + Pricing
- Our Story
- Meet the Team
- How It Works
- Safety & Trust
- FAQ
- Contact

### Core feature requirements

- Sticky header with persistent primary CTA:
  `Book a Walk`
- Click-to-call phone access on mobile and desktop
- Clear service area messaging for Williamsburg and nearby Brooklyn neighborhoods
- Transparent pricing table for all current services
- Real customer-facing photography only
- Trust section with certifications, years in business, and walker consistency messaging
- Testimonials surfaced on key conversion pages, not isolated to one page
- Simple contact/consultation form with minimal required fields
- Strong mobile navigation and responsive layouts across all pages

### Content requirements

- Explain the Not The Rug name and brand story
- Introduce Luis and the business history in Williamsburg
- Highlight differentiators:
  no pack walks beyond stated limits, GPS tracking, double-leashing, NAPPS certification, CPR/First Aid, free consultation
- Add more personality to walker bios without losing professionalism
- Clarify service details, visit lengths, and who each service is for

### UX requirements

- Primary CTA visible without scrolling on homepage
- Service/pricing information accessible in one click from main navigation
- Contact paths limited to high-intent actions:
  call, book, request consultation
- Fast-loading pages with clean visual hierarchy and readable type on mobile

### Definition of done

- A new visitor can understand services, prices, trust signals, and next steps within 60 seconds
- All core pages are complete and internally linked
- The site works cleanly on mobile, tablet, and desktop
- Contact and CTA flows are functional

## Phase 2: Booking and Operations Integration

Objective: remove manual scheduling friction and support recurring revenue with a more professional client experience.

### Required features

- Integrate an online booking/client management platform such as Time To Pet
- Add a clear booking flow for new client consultation requests
- Add client-friendly scheduling for approved existing customers
- Support online payments and invoicing through the selected platform
- Show sample walk-report expectations:
  GPS tracking, photos, visit summaries

### Functional requirements

- Website CTAs route users into the booking platform or consultation workflow
- Booking flow clearly explains onboarding steps before the first walk
- Cancellation, weather, vaccination, and emergency policies are visible before submission
- Form submissions capture enough detail to qualify the lead without creating drop-off
- Analytics events track CTA clicks, form starts, form completions, and booking completions

### Business requirements

- Preserve transparent pricing while encouraging recurring packages
- Position subscriptions/packages as preferred options rather than hiding them
- Reduce reliance on phone and email for routine scheduling

### Definition of done

- A new client can request service online without manual back-and-forth
- Existing clients have a clear path to ongoing scheduling/payment
- Core conversion events are measurable

## Phase 3: Local SEO Expansion

Objective: capture more high-intent search traffic from Brooklyn neighborhoods and service-specific queries.

### Required pages

- Williamsburg dog walking page
- Greenpoint dog walking page
- Bushwick dog walking page
- East Williamsburg dog walking page
- Additional neighborhood pages as service coverage allows

### SEO feature requirements

- Unique localized copy for every neighborhood page
- Service schema and local business schema markup
- Consistent NAP details across the site
- Internal linking from homepage, services, blog, and contact pages to neighborhood pages
- FAQ content aligned to local search intent
- Review content integrated into relevant pages

### Content requirements

- Include local dog parks, common walking routes, and neighborhood context
- Create blog topics tied to seasonal and hyperlocal pet-owner needs
- Build copy around real service advantages over app-based competitors without sounding generic or hostile

### Definition of done

- Each target neighborhood has a distinct landing page with unique value and local relevance
- Technical local SEO basics are implemented
- The site structure supports continued content publishing

## Phase 4: Retention, Referrals, and Growth

Objective: turn the site from a brochure and booking tool into a repeatable growth channel.

### Required features

- Package/subscription presentation with branded tier naming
- Email capture for seasonal tips, service updates, or offers
- Review request and testimonial collection workflow
- Partner/referral content for vets, groomers, trainers, and pet businesses
- Blog/content publishing workflow
- Social content integration plan for Instagram and TikTok

### Growth requirements

- Make recurring walk plans feel like the default purchase path
- Support referral-based acquisition with clear partner and customer prompts
- Reuse site content for short-form social content and neighborhood discovery
- Create repeatable templates for blog posts, landing pages, and testimonials

### Definition of done

- The site supports retention and referral loops, not just first-touch acquisition
- Marketing content can be published without redesigning new pages each time

## Backlog / Nice-to-Have Features

These should not block launch:

- Dynamic pricing calculator
- Loyalty or rewards program
- Real-time response-time widget
- Live GPS preview before signup
- Building-specific landing pages for high-value residences
- Downloadable first-visit checklist or new-client guide
- Advanced CRM/email segmentation

## Non-Functional Requirements

- Mobile-first responsive design
- Accessible navigation, forms, and readable contrast
- Clean performance on modern mobile devices
- CMS/editor workflow that allows content updates without developer involvement
- Analytics and search console configured at launch
- SEO-friendly page structure, metadata, and headings

## Content and Asset Inputs Needed

- Real photography of walkers and dogs
- Updated team bios and headshots
- Finalized insurance/bonding language
- Certification badges and trust assets
- Service area confirmation by neighborhood
- Current contact and booking policies
- Testimonials approved for reuse

## Recommended Delivery Sequence

1. Complete Phase 1 content, page structure, and visual design.
2. Implement Phase 2 booking integration and conversion tracking.
3. Publish Phase 3 neighborhood pages and local SEO foundation.
4. Layer in Phase 4 retention, referral, and content-growth systems.

## Success Metrics

- Increase consultation requests and completed bookings
- Reduce drop-off between site visit and first contact
- Increase recurring package adoption
- Improve rankings for neighborhood dog-walking searches
- Grow review volume and branded search demand
