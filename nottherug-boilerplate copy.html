The Process

How it works

From first contact to daily walks — here's exactly what to expect when you join Not The Rug.

01

Reach Out

Fill out our simple intake form or give us a call. Tell us about your dog — breed, age, personality, any quirks we should know about. We respond within 2 hours on weekdays.

02

Free Meet & Greet

We come to your home. Your dog meets their future walker in their own space, on their own terms. We discuss your routine, review key handling, and answer every question. No charge, no commitment.

03

Set Up Your Profile

We'll log feeding schedules, vet contacts, emergency protocols, door codes, and any behavioral notes. Your dog's profile travels with their walker on every visit.

04

First Walk

Your assigned walker arrives within a 15-minute window. GPS tracking starts, your dog gets 45 minutes of proper exercise, and you receive a photo report when they're home safe.

05

Ongoing & Recurring

Same walker, same time, same route calibrated to your dog's preferences. Monthly invoicing, simple cancellation policy (48-hour notice), and an open line to us whenever you need it.

Our Story

14 years of walks,
one neighborhood

Not The Rug was born in Williamsburg and has never left. Here's why that matters.

Founded 2011

A neighborhood service, not a platform

Not The Rug was founded in 2011 by Luis, a longtime Williamsburg resident who started walking dogs for neighbors while building a career in music and event coordination. What began as a favor for a few friends on N 7th Street became Brooklyn's most trusted neighborhood dog walking service.

The name is a promise. Your dog will not ruin your rug because they'll be properly walked, genuinely cared for, and returned home happy. It's also a nod to the neighborhood's sense of humor — we don't take ourselves too seriously, but we take your dog very seriously.

We've never expanded beyond what we can do well. We don't use gig workers. We don't dispatch strangers. Every walker on our team has been with us for years, knows the neighborhood by heart, and knows your dog by name.

How We Work

The principles behind every walk

01

Consistency Over Convenience

We turn down more clients than we take on. Not because we're exclusive — because we only take new clients when we can assign a consistent walker who has true capacity. Your dog doesn't need a different person every week.

02

Small Groups, Real Attention

Three dogs maximum per walk. Always. That's not a marketing line — it's a practical commitment to safe, attentive care. Your dog gets exercise and engagement, not crowd management.

03

Neighborhood Expertise

We know which parks get flooded in rain, which blocks have aggressive off-leash dogs, which routes are best for reactive dogs, and which shortcuts to use in summer heat. 14 years builds that kind of knowledge.

04

Real People, Always Reachable

Luis's personal number is on the website. You can text or call your walker directly. There's no support ticket system here. If something happens — good or bad — you hear from a human, immediately.

Safety & Trust

Why trust matters
more than price

Every trust and safety standard we hold ourselves to — and why we hold it.

🛡️

Fully Insured & Bonded

Not The Rug carries comprehensive pet care liability insurance and is fully bonded. In the unlikely event of an accident or property issue, you're protected. We'll share proof of insurance on request.

✅

Background-Checked Team

Every member of our team undergoes a comprehensive background check before their first walk. We vet our walkers as carefully as you'd vet someone with a key to your home — because that's exactly what they have.

📍

GPS Tracking on Every Walk

Every walk is GPS logged. You receive a post-walk route map showing exactly where your dog went, how long they walked, and when they returned. No guessing, no vague check-ins.

🔐

Double-Leash Safety Method

All our walkers use our signature dual collar-and-harness method on every walk. Two points of contact means if one fails, your dog is still safe. This is non-negotiable regardless of breed or temperament.

🚑

CPR & First Aid Certified

Our entire active team is certified in pet CPR and first aid. We also maintain current contact for each client's vet and have an emergency protocol reviewed at every onboarding.

👥

Max 3 Dogs Per Walk

We cap every group walk at three dogs. This is a safety standard and a quality standard. Your dog gets genuine attention — not a chaotic pack of strangers that can't be safely managed.

Common Questions

What families usually ask

What happens if my dog gets injured on a walk? +

We contact you immediately, administer first aid if needed, and transport to your designated vet or the nearest emergency clinic. Our insurance covers veterinary costs arising from walker negligence. We document everything and stay with your dog until you can be there.

Will my dog always have the same walker? +

Yes, in the vast majority of cases. We assign a primary walker at onboarding and only introduce a backup walker (who you'll meet in advance) if your regular walker is unavailable. We never send an unknown person to your home.

What are your vaccination requirements? +

All dogs must be current on Rabies, DHPP (distemper/parvo), and Bordetella vaccines. We require documentation at onboarding. This protects your dog, our walkers, and other dogs in our care.

What's your cancellation policy? +

48-hour notice for individual walks, no charge. For boarding, we request 72-hour notice for full refunds. We understand life is unpredictable and handle edge cases with flexibility.
